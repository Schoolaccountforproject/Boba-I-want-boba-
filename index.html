Pearl Tapioca

Tapioca pearls are characteristically soft and chewy, with a prominent elastic texture and translucent appearance. They are made with tapioca starch (or starch from the cassava plant). Boba pearls are usually sold dry, and then cooked in water until soft. Store-bought boba pearls also have other additions to help keep their shape and prolong shelf life.

Pudding

Pudding, any of several foods whose common characteristic is a relatively soft, spongy, and thick texture. There are different types of puddings for boba toppings including but not limited to egg pudding, different types of fruits puddings, tapioca puddings, etc.

Cheese Foam

Cheese foam is a thick, creamy, velvety foam that's typically used as a topping for iced tea, boba drinks, or coffee. If you're a fan of whipped cream or cheese cream, you'll love this topping because it is made by whipping heavy cream or whipping cream cheese with milk, sugar, and salt, and the texture is similar to cappuccino foam but not as stiff as whipped cream.

Fruits

There are a lot of fruit milk tea but you are also encouraged to add fruits as toppings in your drinks. Fruits contains numerous nutritous elements that benefits human body so adding fruits in your boba would be a great substitution for other artifical sweeteners like cheese foam or pearls.

Jelly

Jelly is often added to boba, also known as bubble tea, to smooth out the tapioca pearls and popping bubbles, and for its health benefits and unique flavor. Fruit jelly is commonly made of coconut meat as well as konjar, a vegetable by-product. This makes the jelly completely vegan as well as offering a fresh sweet flavour to your bubble tea. It promotes:

  1. Blood sugar control
  2. Better heart health
  3. Weight loss and better weight management
  4. Boosts to skin health
  5. Relief from IBS (irritable bowel syndrome)
